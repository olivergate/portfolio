Founding Engineer (Generalist) — pre-seed, remote

We are a 3-person team building a productivity tool for knowledge workers. Backed by
angel investors out of NYC and London. The first version is in beta with ~200 weekly
active users; we're hiring our first engineer hire to take ownership of the stack
end-to-end so the founders can focus on product distribution.

You'll work directly with the technical co-founder. The work is broad: frontend
features, backend APIs, basic infra, customer-facing bug triage, and occasional
product/design contributions. We don't have a dedicated designer or PM — you'll read
support tickets, talk to users, and decide what to ship.

Stack: Next.js + TypeScript on the frontend, Node.js + Postgres on the backend, Vercel
for hosting. We use Linear, Slack, Notion. Greenfield enough that you can swap pieces
out if you have strong opinions.

You should:

- Be comfortable owning a feature from "we should probably build this" to production
- Have shipped at least one full-stack web product end-to-end (React + a real backend)
- Be unfazed by ambiguity — the spec is rarely complete, the data is rarely clean
- Read code outside your home language (Python, Go, etc.) without complaining
- Communicate clearly in writing — most decisions happen async
- Be able to context-switch between bug fixing, building, and customer triage

Bonus:

- Experience working at a 1–10 person startup before
- Taste in UI/UX (we don't have a designer)
- Comfort with LLM-assisted development workflows
- Production experience with a database other than Postgres
- Have done customer support directly with end users at any point

What we offer:

- Meaningful equity (early enough to matter)
- Remote-first, async-first culture
- Direct line to the founders and the early users
- No HR, no managers, no roadmap meetings — you ship things
